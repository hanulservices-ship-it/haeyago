Break-even Calculator

Calculate how many units you need to sell before your business starts making a profit.

Enter your costs, then tap Calculate.

How to Use

Step 1: Enter Fixed Costs

Enter expenses that remain the same regardless of sales, such as rent, salaries, utilities, subscriptions, and insurance.

Step 2: Enter Selling Price per Unit

Enter the amount you charge customers for one product or service.

Step 3: Enter Variable Cost per Unit

Enter the cost required to produce or deliver one unit, such as materials, packaging, or commissions.

Step 4: Tap Calculate

The calculator will show the minimum number of units you need to sell to recover all your costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a break-even point?

The break-even point is the number of units you must sell before your total revenue equals your total costs.

Why is it important?

Knowing your break-even point helps you set realistic sales goals and pricing strategies.

Can I use this calculator for services?

Yes. It works for products, services, consulting, freelance work, and subscriptions.

What happens after the break-even point?

Every additional sale beyond the break-even point contributes to profit, assuming your costs remain the same.

Formula

Break-even Units

Break-even = Fixed Costs ÷ (Selling Price − Variable Cost)

Example

Fixed Costs = ₱10,000
Selling Price = ₱500
Variable Cost = ₱300

Break-even Units = 50
