💡 Tips

  • Shipping companies usually charge whichever is higher: the Actual Weight or the Volumetric Weight.
  • Using a smaller box can reduce shipping costs.
  • Different couriers use different volumetric divisors.
  • Always check your courier's latest shipping policy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is volumetric weight?

Volumetric (or dimensional) weight is a shipping weight calculated from the size of a package instead of its actual weight.

Why is my chargeable weight higher?

Couriers charge whichever is greater between the actual weight and the volumetric weight.

What divisor should I use?

Many air cargo services use 5000, many international couriers use 6000, while some freight forwarders use 4000. Always verify with your shipping provider.
