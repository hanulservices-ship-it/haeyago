Markup Calculator

Calculate your markup percentage and profit using your cost price and selling price.

Enter your Cost Price and Selling Price, then tap Calculate.

How to Use

Step 1: Enter Your Cost Price

Enter the amount you paid to purchase or produce your product.

Step 2: Enter Your Selling Price

Enter the price you are charging your customer.

Step 3: Tap Calculate

The calculator will instantly show your Profit and Markup Percentage.

Example

If your Cost Price is ₱100 and your Selling Price is ₱140, your Profit is ₱40 and your Markup is 40%.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is markup?

Markup is the percentage added to your cost price to determine your selling price.

Why is markup important?

Markup helps ensure you earn enough profit to cover business expenses and generate income.

Can markup be negative?

Yes. If your selling price is lower than your cost price, the markup becomes negative, meaning you are selling at a loss.

Is markup the same as profit margin?

No. Markup is calculated from the cost price, while profit margin is calculated from the selling price.

Formula

Profit

Profit = Selling Price − Cost Price

Markup (%)

Markup = (Profit ÷ Cost Price) × 100

Example

Cost Price = ₱100
Selling Price = ₱140

Profit = ₱40
Markup = 40.00%
